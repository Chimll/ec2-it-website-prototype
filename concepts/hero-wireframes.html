Hero page layout concepts

These are low-to-mid fidelity homepage hero options for a neutral, practical London MSP website. The aim is to show page structure and message priority, not final artwork.

Client-first messaging Neutral tone City-focused proof Clear next step No slider or parallax

Concept 01

Challenge-led confidence

Best when the homepage needs to speak to referred prospects who are already wondering whether their IT setup is slowing the business down.

  • Leads with the client's situation, not the supplier.
  • Shows what an IT review covers before asking for an enquiry.
  • Keeps package navigation visible without making pricing the lead story.

Concept 02

Question-led entry point

Best when the homepage should feel more useful immediately, with visitors choosing the issue that brought them to the site.

  • Supports the meeting note: "six ways to improve your IT".
  • Frames EC2 IT around client triggers rather than services.
  • Works well with Knowledge Hub and case study routes.

Concept 03

Proof-led City story

Best when EC2 IT wants to make the page feel established and local while keeping claims grounded in case studies and practical outcomes.

  • Gives case studies prominence above the fold.
  • Makes the City focus visible without relying on stock imagery.
  • Lets the rest of the page expand into challenges, packages and FAQs.

Concept 04

Role-led reassurance

Best when the homepage needs to serve business owners, operations managers and internal IT contacts without making the hero too long.

  • Creates immediate relevance for different buyer roles.
  • Supports the 5:1 client-to-us content direction.
  • Can reuse Joomla articles or modules behind each role panel.
